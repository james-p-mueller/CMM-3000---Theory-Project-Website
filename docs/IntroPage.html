Introduction to RDT

Section Author - James Mueller

The basic definition of relational dialectics has a few components. First, that the words and phrases we use inherently carry meaning, and that second, the meaning applied to those words by people may be different from person to person. Furthermore, when two parties have a conversation, the words and phrases are not said in a random order, but instead we might first exchange a greeting before talking about how we feel, or something going on in your life.
