RDT in College

Section Author - James Mueller

RDT plays a role in each of our lives even as college students. Students are always operating under multiple and unique stressors, whether it be assignments, work, finding an internship, etc. These are just a few of the standout examples that were easiest to point to. The development of friendships is a fundamental and important part of a succesful college experience, so understanding more about how our environment influences the choices we make is very important.

Privacy VS Connection: Finding a balance between work, classes, and having a social life can be hard. Many college students spend nights out having fun growing their social connections as well as maintaining their social health. We also need some nights to ourselves, as we need to rest and recover.

Sameness VS Change: During the first few weeks of everyone's college experience, we all looked for new people to spend time with and things to do, whether it was on campus or not. Usually after a certain amount of time, people settled into their friendships and relationships and tended to socialize primarily with them. At the beginining of each new semester as we meet new people, this group of people would grow, shrink, and change.

Openeness VS Closedness: People will decide to disclose certain things about themselves depending on who they are with and other circumstances as well. Certain topics of conversation may come up depending on what class you are in.

Involvement VS Distance: We choose to spend our time with certain people and doing certain activities. many college students find a new interest or group of people to spend time with through thier classes or clubs. This is an active choice that we make.
