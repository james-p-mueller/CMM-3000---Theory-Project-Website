Future of RDT

Section Author - James Mueller

RDT is still a developing idea in the communications because of the complex nature of human relationships. As each individual will have unique conceptions going into any given interaction, it is extremely hard to create a "control" for studying RDT.


One leading researcher in the field of RDT is Dr. Suter. In a paper published in the Journal of Family Communication called Charting a Research Agenda for Relational Dialectics Theory: Forwarding Critical Theorizing in Interpersonal and Family Communication Research suggests that "Advancements might also be made by putting RDT in conversation with other contemporary critical theoretical developments... [two studies] Sacs (2023), and Tian (2024) serve as two recent examples. Sachs... merits recognition again given its integration of queer theorizing and RDT. Likewise, Tian integrates RDT with new materialist perspectives. Using both RDT and agential realism theory as heuristics, Tian analyzes intersecting occupational, gender, and familial identities of Chinese female entrepreneurs." (Suter 189).


There are still hundreds of avenues with which RDT needs to be explored, Suter themself pointing to two previous studies to use as guideposts for future researchers of RDT. Factors such sexuality, gender identity, race, class, occupation, and many other factors all need to be explored to greater degrees. Given how the unique identities of those involved in any given study of RDT will innevitabley influence the results of the study to some extent inherently, it is still important to study the way these factors intermingle.
